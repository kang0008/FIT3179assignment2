Road Safety and Financial Cost in Victoria: Decade Overview




With the Victorian roads being filled with various types of cars driven by people across various ages, road safety and financial costs arising from using the cars had been one of the biggest reasons why some Victorians are choosing not to drive.In this visualisation, we explore whether such concerns had persisted through time...

Average Cars per Household (2016)












Households outside of metropolitan area generally have more cars than households in metropolitan area. Due to the city having a higher density and various public transportation, the financial costs far outweights the benefits of owning a car. Car owners in Melbourne has an average spend of $20,856.73 per year. Meanwhile, Victoria regional areas has a significantly lower running cost of $13,601.50 per year.
